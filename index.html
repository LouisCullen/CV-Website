Experience

Cloud Architecture DevOps

My experience mainly comes from shadow work with Cossy Cosmas of Diebold Nixdorf. I worked with Cossy across a six moth period, followed by occasional meetings which continue to the present. During these sessions, we discuss not only the skills which are required to convert university education to industrial applications, but also his responsibilities in the company and how they utilise cloud computing to their advantage. The focuses include:

  • Understanding the significant financial, operational and environmental benefits of cloud computing.
  • Reconciling my theoretical understanding of AWS Cloud technologies with direct exposure to a fully operational PCI DSS transaction processing service.
  • Exploring the pros and cons of a strict DevOps deployment methodology.
  • Developing a deeper understanding of DevOps concepts such as Infrastructure as Code and Configuration Management using Terraform and Puppet applications.
  • Learning coding concepts such as code branching, peer review, merging to master and deployment using GIT based code management tools.
  • Gaining an insight in to best-practice automation tools such as Jenkins and techniques such as Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment.
  • Discussing emerging cloud technologies such as serverless computing and what benefits they bring to large organisations.

From these meetings, I have developed a greater understanding of how theoretical concepts, some of which I have covered in university modules, can be applied to a functioning company and be conveyed to less technical teams. The process of converting concepts to presentations to features is undoubtedly an important part of the development cycle, which I now have a greater understanding of in an industrial setting.
